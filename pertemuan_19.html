Naruto Uzumaki

Naruto[a] is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Masashi Kishimoto. It tells the story of Naruto Uzumaki, a young, socially isolated ninja who seeks recognition from his peers and dreams of becoming the Hokage, the leader of his village. The story is told in two parts: the first is set in Naruto's pre-teen years (volumes 1-27), and the second in his teens (volumes 28-72). The series is based on two one-shot manga by Kishimoto: Karakuri (1995), which earned Kishimoto an honorable mention in Shueisha's monthly Hop Step Award the following year, and Naruto (1997).

Sasuke Uchiha

Sasuke Uchiha (Japanese: うちは サスケ, Hepburn: Uchiha Sasuke) is a fictional character in the Naruto manga and anime series created by Masashi Kishimoto. He is one of the last surviving members of Konohagakure's Uchiha clan, a once-powerful ninja clan that was massacred by his older brother, Itachi Uchiha. Sasuke is initially introduced as a member of Team 7, alongside Naruto Uzumaki and Sakura Haruno, under the leadership of Kakashi Hatake. Throughout the series, Sasuke's primary goal is to avenge his clan by killing Itachi, which leads him down a dark path as he seeks power at any cost.
